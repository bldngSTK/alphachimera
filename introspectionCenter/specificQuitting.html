Unwittingly Quitting



Sometimes we 'unwittingly quit', or give up on something because we're not aware of another way forward. This oftentimes leads to us heavily regretting our action of when we gave up, and also may cause others to feel disappointed that we quit when we could've still tried. All human beings are capable of experiencing emotions because all emotions exist to provide useful information in some way, and Anger is a protective response against some form of hurt. The more you address the root 'hurt' that is causing the anger, the more the anger starts to decompress.

What are you angry about?



Ex: I'm angry about being fired from my job.

How were you hurt in some way?



Ex: Getting fired hurts because I really wanted
to keep my job and I got wrongly fired.
