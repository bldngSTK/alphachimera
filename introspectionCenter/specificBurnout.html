Mitigate Burnout

Burnout is different from standard tiredness, stress, 'laziness', and feeling bad because burnout is always work-related and arises from 6 synergistic hybrid-problems that are caused by the intersection of the individual (controllable) and the workplace (influencable). Burnout makes us have a lack of energy (low excitement, time feels flat), lack of empathy (stops caring about others, fatalistically cynicical remarks/beliefs), and a lack of enjoyment (job doesn't feel fulfilling). 'Burnout' also implies a sense of 'permanently drained', but a discrepancy between an individual and the workplace in 6 different factors cause burnout and resolving these 6 discrepancy factors causes the burnout to naturally melt away.

Burnout-Inducing Factors You Workplace
Workload
Discrepancy between how much you can
work without being overworked and how
much more you actually work
How much are you willing to work
and how much does your workplace expect you to work?


Ex: My workplace expects me to work 40 hours a week
but I'm a full-time student willing to work 20 hours a week.
What desired outcome do you want making you voluntarily overwork yourself?


Ex: 'My boss will like me', 'I'll get more money', 'This desperately needs to get done', etc.
Control
Discrepancy between how much control
you want and how much control you have
How can you be a control freak for everything to happen in
a particular way that clashes with the workplace's consideration?


Ex: I want everyone to wash their hands for 30 seconds
anytime they enter/leave the kitchen, any less frustrates me.
How do people at your workplace micro-manage you to
work their way while you want to work a different way?


Ex: My boss frustrates me be micro-managing everything, from the email's font-size to my desk's organization.
Reward
Discrepancy between what reward you want
and what the workplace provides
How are you inappropriately expecting an unfairly high compensation
by losing sight of what's really important with the reward?


Ex: I want a higher salary because I am egotistically comparing my
salary/bonuses with other employees and I want to feel better than my peers.
How is the workplace underpaying you for how much you're trying?


Ex: I'm not getting payed for all my work (like overtime) because I'm working full-time instead of contract.
Social
Discrepancy between how connected
to the workplace community you
feel and how isolated you are.
How do you cause yourself isolation at your workplace?


Ex: I isolate myself from my peers because I am terrified of being
scrutinized by them, and I also judge them thinking they're bad people.
How does the workplace make you spend too much time isolated?


Ex: My programming job is 'solo-work heavy' and instead of 'group heavy', and
there's toxic people everywhere, making me isolated and not feel connected.
Fairness
Discrepancy between how well you
feel you're treated and feeling like
you're being treated unfairly.
How do you yourself influence 'feeling unfairly treated at work',
by either 'feeling like things are unfair even though they aren't'
or tolerating/reinforcing/cultivating an unfair environment?


Ex: My sense of unfairness is caused by me due to my willingness to be a doormat by not
advocating for myself when things really are unfair, because I want people to not hate me
How is the workplace actually unfair and cause you to
get cynical where despite you and someone else having the same
effect at work, their yield is much greater than your yield?


Ex: The culture of fairness at the workplace is very poor because people
play politic, and there is a lot of nepotism/unfairness against me.
Value
Discrepancy between the workplace's
values and your own values.
How do your values go against what the company values
(perhaps needing you to appropriately re-shift values)?


Ex: I am flipping burgers to make ends meet with my family, but when my workplace
asks me to work over-time to satisfy more customers and
takes away time I can spend with my family, I feel burnt out
How does the workplace make you sacrifice your values for their values,
making you feel less rewarded by the job due to them outweighing
why you show up with why they want you?


Ex: The reason I became a doctor is because I value saving lives, but when my
workplace spends hours on billing and insurance-company calls, the quality of hours I work feels lesser.
