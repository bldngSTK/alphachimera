Excessive Daydreaming



Anger is different from other emotions because most negative emotions are usually socially acceptable to express ('Sad people who break down crying deserve sympathy', 'Worried people are panicking because they care', etc) but people who explode in Anger (after trying to repeatedly suppress it) often get blamed, demonized, villified, and frowned upon the most, because it's easiest to see the damaging effects of Anger (even though other emotions cause damage too). All human beings are capable of experiencing emotions because all emotions exist to provide useful information in some way, and Anger is a protective response against some form of hurt. The more you address the root 'hurt' that is causing the anger, the more the anger starts to decompress.

What are you angry about?



Ex: I'm angry about being fired from my job.

How were you hurt in some way?



Ex: Getting fired hurts because I really wanted
to keep my job and I got wrongly fired.
